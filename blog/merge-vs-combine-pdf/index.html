Merge vs Combine PDFs — Understanding the Terminology

Clearing up the confusion between merging, combining, appending, and assembling PDF files — what each term means and which approach you actually need.

If you need to assemble reports, start with merge PDF files. To remove pages first, use split PDF tools.

Is There a Difference?

In the context of PDF tools, "merge" and "combine" are generally used interchangeably. Both terms refer to the process of taking two or more separate PDF files and joining them sequentially into a single, cohesive document.

Common Use Cases for Merging

Combining files is essential in many workflows. For instance, you might merge individual scanned pages into a single contract, combine multiple project reports into an end-of-year summary, or append an appendix to an existing research paper.

Things to Watch Out For

When merging documents, be mindful of page sizes and orientations. If you combine a landscape presentation with a portrait letter-sized document, the final PDF will contain pages of varying dimensions. Additionally, merging large files will result in a larger final document, which may affect email shareability.
