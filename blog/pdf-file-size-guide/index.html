Why PDFs Get So Big

The most common culprit for bloated PDF file sizes is unoptimized imagery. High-resolution photos or graphics saved without compression can cause a file to balloon to dozens or hundreds of megabytes. Embedded fonts and complex vector data can also contribute to the overall size.

Methods for Reducing Size

To shrink a PDF, you typically compress the images within it, lowering their DPI (dots per inch) and increasing JPEG compression. Another method is to subset fonts, meaning only the specific characters used in the document are saved, rather than the entire font family.

The Quality Trade-off

When reducing file size, there is always a trade-off with visual quality. Aggressive compression can lead to blurry images and artifacts. It's important to find a balance where the file is small enough to easily email or upload, but the content remains legible and professional.
